# Cardio Basics for Beginners

## Goal

Provide low-risk cardio guidance for beginner plans that need simple intensity
control.

## Intensity rules

- Low to moderate intensity is the default starting point for deconditioned
  users.
- A conversational pace is usually a safer default than maximal interval work.
- Session difficulty should match the user's training history, available time,
  and recovery capacity.

## Common modalities

- Walking
- Cycling
- Elliptical training
- Marching in place
- Low-impact circuits with short work periods

## Session design

- Short sessions can start around 10 to 20 minutes and extend over time.
- Cardio can be standalone or placed after strength work when fatigue does not
  compromise safety.
- Intervals for beginners should use generous recovery periods and avoid
  all-out sprint language.

## Safety constraints

- Stop sessions for chest pain, severe breathlessness, faintness, or unusual
  symptoms.
- Low-impact options are preferred when joint irritation is already present.
