# Warm-Up and Cool-Down Basics

## Goal

Support plan sections that prepare users for training and end sessions gradually.

## Warm-up guidance

- Warm-ups should raise body temperature and rehearse the session's movement
  patterns.
- Five to ten minutes is often enough for beginner sessions.
- Dynamic movements are usually a better fit before training than long passive
  stretching blocks.

## Practical warm-up options

- Brisk walking
- Marching in place
- Arm circles
- Bodyweight squats to a comfortable depth
- Light band rows or shoulder activation drills

## Cool-down guidance

- Cool-downs can gradually reduce effort and reinforce comfortable range of motion.
- Easy walking and gentle stretching are usually sufficient for beginner plans.
- Cool-downs should not introduce painful or advanced mobility drills.
