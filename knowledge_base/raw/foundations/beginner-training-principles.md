# Beginner Training Principles

## Goal

Provide stable beginner guidance that helps the planner choose a manageable
weekly structure instead of an aggressive plan.

## Core principles

- Most beginners respond well to 2 to 4 training days per week.
- Full-body training is usually simpler to recover from than high-volume split
  routines.
- Start with moderate effort and leave a small performance buffer instead of
  pushing every set to failure.
- Use a mix of squat, hinge, push, pull, carry, and core patterns across the
  week when equipment allows.
- Increase difficulty gradually through small jumps in reps, time, load, or
  session density.

## Programming heuristics

- Choose fewer exercises done consistently rather than many exercises rotated
  randomly.
- Match session length to the user's actual schedule so adherence stays realistic.
- Beginners benefit from repeating the same main patterns long enough to learn
  technique and confidence.
- Rest days should be spread across the week when training frequency increases.

## Safety constraints

- Sharp pain, dizziness, chest pain, or severe shortness of breath are stop
  signals, not adaptation signals.
- Existing injuries or movement limitations should reduce exercise complexity and
  range of motion demands.
