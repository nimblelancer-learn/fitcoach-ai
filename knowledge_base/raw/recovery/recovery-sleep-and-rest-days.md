# Recovery, Sleep, and Rest Days

## Goal

Keep generated plans realistic by treating recovery as part of the program, not
as an optional extra.

## Recovery heuristics

- Beginners usually need at least one full rest day each week.
- Poor sleep, persistent soreness, and declining motivation can signal that
  total workload is too high.
- Recovery improves when hard sessions are separated by easier days or rest days.

## Sleep guidance

- Consistent sleep schedules support energy, recovery, and training adherence.
- Reduced sleep is a reason to scale intensity or volume down temporarily.

## Plan adjustment rules

- If soreness lasts several days, reduce sets, intensity, or exercise novelty.
- If a user misses sessions often, simplify the schedule before adding more work.
- Deload-style weeks can be useful when fatigue accumulates across several weeks.
